
# Platform Architecture
## Demokritos Scientific Journal

Demokritos Scientific Journal is designed as a modern scientific publishing platform that combines traditional peer-reviewed publication with advanced research discovery tools.

The platform integrates manuscript submission, peer review, DOI management, equation indexing, and a physics knowledge graph into a unified system.

---

## System Overview

The platform consists of five major components:

1. Submission and Peer Review System  
2. Publication and DOI Management  
3. Research Discovery Infrastructure  
4. Physics Knowledge Graph  
5. Community and Fellowship Programs  

---

## Submission and Peer Review System

Authors submit manuscripts through a structured workflow.

### Submission Inputs

Authors may submit:

- PDF manuscripts  
- LaTeX source files  
- figures and supplementary materials  
- arXiv identifiers (optional)

### Review Workflow

Submission → Editorial Screening → Peer Review → Revision → Acceptance → Publication

---

## Constructive Peer Review Model

Demokritos Scientific Journal emphasizes **constructive peer review**.

Key principles:

- reviewers are known to authors  
- reviewers focus on improving the research  
- revisions are encouraged whenever possible  

The goal is to strengthen scientific work rather than reject papers prematurely.

---

## Publication and DOI Management

The journal supports two publication paths.

### Journal DOI

Authors may request a DOI issued through the journal.

- Fee: **$25**
- DOI issued through the journal
- Article published on the journal platform

### Zenodo DOI (Free Option)

Authors may publish using a **Zenodo DOI**.

- Fee: **$0**
- DOI provided through Zenodo
- Article published on the journal platform

This ensures that all researchers have a publication path.

---

## Research Discovery Infrastructure

The platform includes discovery tools that help researchers navigate scientific literature.

Examples include:

- citation network explorer  
- equation search engine  
- topic discovery maps  

These tools help researchers find related work more easily.

---

## Equation Index of Physics

The platform extracts LaTeX equations from manuscripts and stores them in a searchable database.

Each equation links to:

- the paper where it appears  
- related topics  
- other papers using similar equations  

This creates a cross-paper searchable index of equations in physics literature.

---

## Physics Knowledge Graph

The journal maintains a knowledge graph linking:

- papers  
- authors  
- equations  
- research topics  
- citations  

Relationships include:

- Paper → cites → Paper  
- Paper → contains → Equation  
- Author → wrote → Paper  

This structure enables powerful research discovery tools.

---

## Young Scientists Section

The journal includes a section dedicated to high-school researchers.

Students may submit original scientific research papers.

Benefits include:

- free publication  
- mentorship-oriented peer review  
- early experience with scientific publishing  

---

## Demokritos Open Research Fellowship

The fellowship program invites participation from:

- independent researchers  
- graduate students  
- early-career scientists  
- high-school researchers  

Fellows collaborate on research projects and participate in the journal’s open science community.

---

## Long-Term Vision

The Demokritos Scientific Journal platform aims to evolve into a global open-science research infrastructure.

Future development includes:

- large-scale equation indexing  
- global research collaboration networks  
- advanced discovery tools for scientific literature
- Self-Driving Quantum Collider Laboratory (SQCL) 

The goal is to transform scientific publishing into a collaborative knowledge ecosystem.
